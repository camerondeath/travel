Shanghai · Apr 3 – 7
Eat like you live here
What to grab, what to order, what to avoid, and how to eat without a plan.
▼
Shanghai’s restaurant culture is well documented. Its street food culture is not — and it’s better. The things worth eating are cheap, require no reservation, and are found by following people who look like they eat there every day. The rule: if there’s a queue of people who are clearly not tourists, join it without knowing what’s at the front.
Before 10 AM — breakfast on the street
Jianbing (煎饼)
The breakfast that defines the city more than any restaurant meal. A thin crepe cracked with egg, smeared with hoisin and chilli paste, wrapped around a crispy wonton skin. Made to order at a cart in 90 seconds. Find them at any residential street corner before 10 AM — after that, the carts pack up. Cost ¥8–12. Order by pointing and holding up fingers. No Mandarin required.
Ci fan tuan (粢饭团) — sticky rice ball
Glutinous rice packed around a fried dough stick, pork floss, and pickled mustard greens. Dense, savoury, intensely filling for ¥8. Locals eat it walking to work. Look for the vendor pressing them in their hands — the youtiao inside gives it a satisfying crunch when you bite through.
Cong you bing (葱油饼) — scallion oil pancake
Flaky layered flatbread with scallions, pan-fried until the outside blisters. ¥6. The best ones have visible layering when torn. Pair with doujiang (soy milk) from the same stall — point at the container, sweet or savoury, nod.
Anytime — no sit-down required
Shengjian bao (生煎包) — pan-fried pork buns
Like a soup dumpling but pan-fried until the base is deeply caramelised and crisp. The filling is pork with a jelly that melts to broth when hot. Eat carefully — they will burn you. A tray of four is ¥12–18. Yang’s Fry-Dumpling near People’s Square is the known institution but the best ones are often at unmarked neighbourhood stalls with a long queue of office workers.
Qingtuan (青团) — green rice dumplings, early April only
A Qingming Festival speciality — glutinous rice dumplings coloured green with wormwood juice, filled with red bean paste or savoury pork. Available at Shen Dacheng (century-old Shanghai confectionery chain) and from street vendors during the holiday Apr 4–6. This is a genuinely seasonal thing you can only get in early April.
Xiaolongbao (小笼包) — soup dumplings, done right
Bite a small hole in the side first, slurp the soup out, then eat the rest. Dip in black Chinkiang vinegar with ginger. The filling should be loose and soupy, not firm. Jia Jia Tang Bao on Huanghe Lu is the local standard-bearer — queue and point, no menu interaction needed.
After 7 PM — follow the smoke
Shaokao (烧烤) — street BBQ
From around 7 PM, charcoal grills appear on pavements across residential neighbourhoods. Skewers of cumin lamb, corn, squid, tofu. Order by pointing and holding up fingers. The cumin lamb is the thing. Cold Tsingtao from the cart beside the grill. This is the version of Shanghai that goes home when tourists go to bed. No map needed — just follow smoke.
Two streets worth knowing
Huanghe Lu (黄河路) — near People’s Square
Old-school food street, recently famous again after featuring in the TV drama Blossoms Shanghai. Old Shanghainese restaurants, scallion pancake stalls, Jia Jia Tang Bao. Loud, no English menus, point at the steamer. Exactly right. Metro Lines 1/2/8 to People’s Square. Map
Friday Muslim Market — outside Huxi Mosque, 1328 Changde Lu
After Friday prayers, a market materialises: Xinjiang lamb kebabs over charcoal, flatbread from a tandoor, nan with sesame. Only on Fridays, only while it lasts. Apr 3 is a Friday — this is on the way between the hotel and the French Concession. Map

Shen’s Garden (沈园)
Song Dynasty garden. The poet Lu You and his first wife Tang Wan, forced to separate, met here once more. He wrote a poem on the wall. Small, quiet, melancholy. ~20 min DiDi. Go early.

The poem Lu You wrote after that meeting, Chai Tou Feng, is one of the most famous in Chinese literature. Tang Wan wrote a response, equally celebrated. The garden carries considerable emotional weight in Chinese culture. Go before tour groups arrive. Map

Lu Xun’s Birthplace Complex (鲁迅故里)
Walkable from Shen’s Garden. Childhood home, Three Flavors Study, surrounding neighbourhood preserved. Allow 90 min.

His childhood home, the private school (Three Flavors Study, 三味书屋), and the surrounding lane-house neighbourhood — all preserved at an intimate scale that Shanghai’s preservation efforts rarely achieve. Coming here before lunch makes the Xianheng meal more interesting. Map

Lunch: Xianheng Hotel (咸亨酒店)
The tavern from Lu Xun’s stories, rebuilt on the original site. Braised pork in Shaoxing wine, fennel broad beans (茴香豆), stinky tofu, warm Huadiao wine. This meal is about place.

The Xianheng appears in several of Lu Xun’s most famous stories. Order the fennel broad beans specifically — they’re the dish from the story. The braised pork belly in Shaoxing wine is the other anchor. Map

You’re full, in a canal town, at the end of a week of cities. Here’s the afternoon —

Return to the hotel and stay in Jinghu Wetland Park. Walk the lake paths, sit by the water, read. After a week of walking cities, the park outside your door may be the best possible use of this afternoon. You don’t have to go anywhere. This is the option the itinerary keeps suggesting and meaning. Take it.

The best trips leave things for next time. Choosing to stop here is not a failure of ambition.

Wupeng Boat (乌篷船) — Shaoxing’s flat-bottomed black-canopied canal gondola. 40-min punted journey through old town waterways. The view from water level is entirely different from walking the same streets. Book at the dock near Shenliquan or the Baita Canal area. Or the Orchid Pavilion (兰亭) — bamboo forest 14km out, where Wang Xizhi wrote the most celebrated calligraphy in Chinese history in 353 AD. DiDi each way.

Boat dock · Orchid Pavilion

Find a Huadiao wine producer and ask to taste from the barrel. Shaoxing is the source of the amber rice wine you’ve been drinking all week — several small producers operate in the old city. Get your DiDi driver to take you to one (show: 黄酒厂 or 绍兴黄酒). This is not on any tourist itinerary. It may not work. The attempt is the point.

Back pocket
No booking required. No fixed time. Turn up when it feels right.
▼
These are the places you go when the itinerary feels like a schedule. None require advance booking. Some are best at specific times — noted where it counts. The whole point is that you can do any of these on impulse, from wherever you already are.
Sit somewhere
Fuxing Park, before 9 AM
Ballroom dancing to a portable speaker, tai chi in the rose garden, chess on stone tables. Locals only. Free. Line 1 to South Shaanxi Rd. Map
Sinan Mansions garden
A courtyard in the French Concession that is somehow quiet despite being four blocks from Huaihai Lu. Free. Sit. Map
Long Bar, Waldorf Astoria, after 5 PM
A genuine 1920s banking hall, strong martinis, no enforced dress code. Prices are high. The room is the reason. The Bund is outside. This is a legitimate place to read for two hours. Map
Look at something
M50 galleries, Moganshan Rd — any weekday morning
120+ galleries in old textile mills on Suzhou Creek. Free to enter. ShanghART is the serious anchor. Map
Propaganda Poster Art Centre — find it
7th floor, residential building, corner of Huashan Lu and Yongjia Lu. Google Maps pin is wrong. 300+ original Chinese propaganda posters 1950–79. Closed Mondays. Approximate map
West Bund waterfront walk
West Bund Museum, new SOM-designed convention centre, Blue Bottle on the water. More interesting architecturally than the main Bund. DiDi 15 min from the Pullman. Map
Eat something with no plan
Huanghe Lu, any time
Old-school food street near People’s Square. No English menus. Point at the steamer. Line 1/2/8 to People’s Square. Map
Zhaozhou Lu noodles, after 9 PM
Late-night noodle street. Stalls that close when the proprietors feel like it. Go and point. Map
Shaokao stall, anywhere, after 7 PM
Follow smoke from any residential neighbourhood. Cumin lamb skewers. Cold Tsingtao. No map needed.
Hear something
JZ Club, Hengshan Rd — evenings
Shanghai’s serious jazz institution. Intimate, real musicians, real crowd. Cover at the door. Map
Black Note, Changhua Lu — late
Jazz loft above an unrelated bar. Turquoise door on the right. Very dark, very good. Goes late. Map
If it’s raining
Shanghai Museum at People’s Square — book via WeChat 12 days out
Free, timed tickets, one of the better collections in China. Tickets open via WeChat at 12:10 PM sharp, gone in 20 minutes. Set an alarm if you want this. Map
West Bund Museum — Fluxus show (ongoing)
Covered and large. The Centre Pompidou collaboration is a serious show. A rainy afternoon here is genuinely well spent. DiDi 15 min from the Pullman. Map
Any longtang lane in the French Concession on a wet morning
Rain makes this neighbourhood look like a different city. The plane trees drip, the stone lane houses go dark and quiet. Anfu Lu or Yongfu Lu — fewer people, better light, the neighbourhood reveals something it keeps to itself on sunny weekends.
