Propaganda Poster Art Centre — find it
300+ original Chinese propaganda posters 1950–79, 7th floor, corner of Huashan Lu and Yongjia Lu. Google Maps pin is wrong. Closed Mondays. Approximate map
Dongtai Lu antique market
Old furniture, clocks, porcelain, Mao memorabilia. You don’t have to buy anything. Map
Eat something with no plan
Huanghe Lu (黄河路), any time
Old-school food street near People’s Square. No English menus. Point at the steamer. Recently famous again from the TV drama Blossoms Shanghai. Map
Jianbing (煎饼) from a street cart, before 10 AM
Thin crepe cracked with egg, smeared with hoisin and chilli, wrapped around a crispy wonton skin. Made in 90 seconds at any residential street corner. ¥8–12. Order by pointing and holding up fingers.
Shaokao (烧烤) street BBQ, after 7 PM
Charcoal grills appear on pavements in residential neighbourhoods from around 7 PM. Cumin lamb skewers, cold Tsingtao. No map needed — follow smoke.
Zhaozhou Lu (肇周路) noodles, after 9 PM
Late-night noodle street in the old city. Stalls that close when the proprietors feel like it. Go and point. Map
Hear something
JZ Club, Hengshan Rd — evenings
Shanghai’s serious jazz institution. Intimate, real musicians, real crowd. Cover at the door. Map
Black Note, Changhua Lu — late
Jazz loft above an unrelated bar. Turquoise door on the right. Very dark, very good. Goes late. Map
If it’s raining
Shanghai Museum at People’s Square — book via WeChat 12 days out
Free, timed tickets. Tickets open via WeChat at 12:10 PM sharp, gone in 20 min. Bronze and ceramics galleries alone are worth a half-day. Map
West Bund Museum — Fluxus show
Covered and large. Centre Pompidou collaboration, a serious show. A rainy afternoon here is well spent. 15 min DiDi from the Pullman. Map
Any longtang lane in the French Concession on a wet morning
Rain makes this neighbourhood look like a different city. Anfu Lu or Yongfu Lu — fewer people, better light. The neighbourhood reveals something it keeps to itself on sunny weekends.
